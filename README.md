## How content was collected

### YouTube transcripts
Research involved identifying key strategic deep-dives from the experts' YouTube channels. Transcripts were extracted to capture the long-form strategy behind their LinkedIn micro-content, focusing on actionable frameworks like "Revenue SEO" and "Demand Gen HIRO models."

### LinkedIn posts
Public post URLs were manually reviewed and archived into markdown. Each file includes the date, core content, and strategic annotation. We focused on "operator content"—frameworks derived from real SaaS work rather than generic motivational posts.

## Emerging themes (playbook seeds)

1. **Founder-led > Company-led:** Personal profiles consistently outperform company pages for trust and reach.
2. **Dark Social is the primary driver:** Buyers hang out in Slack, DMs, and LinkedIn feeds; attribution must account for these untracked channels.
3. **Distribution is the bottleneck:** Most SaaS companies over-invest in creation and under-invest in distributing insights.
4. **Positioning against the Status Quo:** The biggest competitor for B2B SaaS isn't another tool; it's the customer's current manual process.
5. **Authenticity as an Algorithm Hack:** Building in public and sharing "the good, the bad, and the ugly" drives higher engagement than polished PR.
6. **Reputation > Vanity Metrics:** High-intent views from a niche audience of decision-makers are worth more than viral reach.
